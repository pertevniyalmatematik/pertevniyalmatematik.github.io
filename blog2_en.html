From the Footsteps of the Past to Today...

Mathematics is the silent witness of human history. From the geometry used in building the pyramids of Ancient Egypt to the number systems that helped calculate the movement of stars in Babylon, every step has brought us closer to the modern world we live in today.

When we solve algebra problems or use an app today, we continue the legacy left by mathematicians who lived thousands of years ago. Pythagoras’ triangles, Euclid’s planes, Archimedes’ quest for infinity... all have become the building blocks of modern mathematics.

The questions asked in the past still echo today: "What is infinity?", "Do numbers really exist?", "Can everything be calculated?"

Mathematics is not just about numbers, but also a journey of the human mind. And this journey is guided by the traces of the past, shaping the future.
