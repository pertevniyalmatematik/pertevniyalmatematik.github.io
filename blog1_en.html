Is It Possible to Build Mathematics from Scratch?

For most people, mathematics is seen as a set of unchanging rules. But have you ever wondered: Is it really possible to build mathematics from scratch, without any prior knowledge? The answer is surprisingly “yes”—but it’s not as simple as it seems.

It All Starts with a Few Definitions and Axioms

Anyone who wants to construct mathematics from scratch must first define what “exists.” Here, logic and set theory come into play. The foundation of modern mathematics usually lies in Zermelo-Fraenkel Set Theory (ZF), along with the Axiom of Choice (C). These are the rules that determine how “sets” work.

These axioms form the foundation of a massive structure to be built upon. Numbers, functions, equations, and even geometry can be defined with the help of these fundamental rules.

Even Numbers Begin with Definitions

For a child, “1, 2, 3...” may seem like natural numbers. But for a mathematician starting from scratch, even these numbers need to be defined. What zero actually is, what one represents, how addition and multiplication are defined—all need to be reconstructed.

One of the most well-known methods used for this is the Peano Axioms. These axioms logically explain natural numbers and their properties. Afterward, natural numbers can be extended to integers, rational numbers, real numbers, and complex numbers.
